⚠ SLEEP DEBT WARNING — READ BEFORE PROCEEDING

On college days (Tue–Sat), this schedule produces ~5–5.5 hours of sleep per night (3:30 AM wake, 9–10 PM sleep). This is below the biological minimum for cognitive function, muscle recovery, and memory consolidation — all three of which you need simultaneously (academics + bulking + coding). § 09 — Strategy
🚆 Train Time Playbook

You spend ~3–3.5 hrs on Bongaon–Sealdah trains every college day. That's 15+ hrs/week. Here is exactly how to use it.

Total train time per week: ~15–17 hours. If used for reading, that's the equivalent of 2 full extra days of study time. If wasted on Reels, that's your CGPA bleeding out every morning. The Bongaon local train is crowded, loud, and uncomfortable. None of those are excuses.
✅
DO on the Morning Train
  • Read today's first subject theory (pre-warm for class)
  • Review yesterday's notes — 10-minute refresh per subject
  • Work through derivations and proofs on paper
  • Write pseudocode for today's lab experiment in your notebook
  • Read FLAT state diagram problems — no internet needed
  • Memorize Biology definitions and mnemonics
  • Review DM proofs — induction, combinatorics
  • Study EVS — complete syllabus fits in 2 train rides
🚫
NEVER on the Train
  • Short-form video (Reels, YouTube Shorts, TikTok)
  • Passive music listening for more than 10 min
  • Social media browsing (Instagram, WhatsApp group chats)
  • Sleeping past 20 minutes on morning train
  • Online shopping or irrelevant browsing
  • New Android coding (no laptop, no IDE, no point)
  • Starting new topics you can't finish in one sitting
📐
Crowd Strategy
  • Standing? Hold book with one hand, read vertically
  • No seat? Use the door-side pole — stable position to read

§ 12 — Non-Negotiables
Laws & Warnings

These are not suggestions. These are the rules this schedule runs on. Break them and the system breaks.

LAW 01 · CRITICAL
3:30 AM is 3:30 AM
There is no version of this schedule where the 3:30 AM alarm is optional. Miss it → miss workout → miss train → miss first class → cascade failure. Install a separate alarm device. Tell a family member to wake you if needed. No snooze protocol means first alarm = up, feet on floor. Staying horizontal after the alarm is the most dangerous action you can take.
LAW 02 · CRITICAL
5:45 AM is Zero-Tolerance
Missing the 6:00 AM auto means missing the 7:30 AM train. There is no next train that gets you to college on time for 9:45 AM. Pack your bag the night before — every night. Clothes laid out the night before. No decisions in the morning. Morning is execution only.
LAW 03 · SLEEP
Sleep Debt is a Real Debt
Running on 5 hrs/night for 5 days straight impairs memory, muscle recovery, and decision-making — all three of which you need simultaneously. You are allowed exactly one bad sleep night per week (Friday). All other nights, target 10 PM lights out. Sunday 8.5 hrs is the weekly repayment. Without it, you will burn out by week 6.
LAW 04 · PHONE
Phone Reels Will Steal Your Career
You have approximately 15 mins of discretionary mobile time in the morning before departure. Every minute on Reels or Instagram is directly subtracted from your workout, your train study, or your sleep. Airplane mode from 10 PM to 6 AM, always. Reels during commute = dead time. 30 minutes of daily Reels = 3.5 hrs/week = the equivalent of your entire Sunday Android block, gone.
LAW 05 · WORKOUT
Workout Feeds the Schedule, Not Fights It
You are in a bulking phase. Skipping workouts does not save time — it costs you. Compound lifts at 4:30 AM raise testosterone, sharpen focus, and set a physiological "on" signal for the rest of the day. The train study window is MORE effective after a morning workout than after just waking up. Do not skip unless actually injured.
LAW 06 · RESOURCES
One Resource Per Subject — Always
Lipschutz for DM. Hopcroft for FLAT. CLRS for DAA. Patterson for CA. Campbell for Bio. Masters for EVS. That is it. Finding a "better" YouTube channel or a "more detailed" PDF is procrastination disguised as preparation. The resource is not the bottleneck. Doing the work is.
LAW 07 · ANDROID
Android Dev is Not Optional
The Monday 7:00–9:30 AM block and Sunday 9:00–11:00 AM block are the engine of your career. Every week you skip the Android roadmap is a week you fall behind the version of yourself who didn't skip. College academics get you the degree. Android dev gets you the job. Both must run simultaneously — no sacrificing one for the other.
LAW 08 · LABS
Lab Records Must Be Done Same Day
Every Thursday and Friday lab session: the record must be completed and signed before leaving college or completed on the return train. Lab backlogs compound — you cannot write 3 records the night before submission. Arriving pre-prepared (pseudocode written on train) means finishing 45 minutes faster in the lab.

Your hardest cognitive work (Android dev, DAA proofs, FLAT derivations) goes in your freshest slots — Monday morning, Sunday morning, train going TO college. Easy work (EVS reading, Bio mnemonics, note review) goes in tired slots — return train, Friday evening. Misaligning this (doing hard work when tired) is the number one reason schedules fail.
